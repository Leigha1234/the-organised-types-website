You Don’t Need Fixing — You Need Support

I used to think that feeling stuck meant I was failing. That I just wasn’t good enough, organised enough, or capable enough. That if I just tried harder, I could solve everything on my own.

That mindset nearly broke me.

Running a business while juggling family life, client demands, and my own personal expectations felt overwhelming. I tried to “keep it together,” carrying every responsibility myself, thinking asking for help was a weakness. I convinced myself I could handle it all — until I hit a wall.

"I wasn’t broken. I wasn’t failing. I was thinking alone."

Why perspective changes everything

When you’re in the middle of it, it’s hard to see clearly. Decisions feel heavier. Tasks feel impossible. Anxiety builds quietly until it feels like it’s controlling your life.

That’s why conversation is so powerful. Even 20 minutes of talking through your overwhelm, with someone who understands, can clarify priorities, break down big tasks, and reduce the mental load.

Practical steps you can take today

  1. Write it down – List everything on your mind. Business, family, personal.
  2. Identify one priority – What’s the one thing that would make the biggest difference?
  3. Take one small step – Even a 10-minute action counts.
  4. Share it – Talk it through with someone you trust.
  5. Reflect and repeat – Build trust in yourself through momentum.
